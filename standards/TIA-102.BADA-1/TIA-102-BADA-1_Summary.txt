TIA-102.BADA-1 Summary
Project 25 Telephone Interconnect Requirements and Definitions (Voice Service)
Addendum 1: Conventional Individual Calls
ANSI/TIA-102.BADA-1 2006 | Approved May 3, 2006
(TIA/EIA-102.BADA-1 Issue D, February 2004)

OVERVIEW

This document is Addendum 1 to TIA/EIA-102.BADA, the base specification for Project 25
telephone interconnect (phone patch) requirements. Where the base document covers both
trunked and conventional P25 systems, this addendum focuses exclusively on improving
telephone interconnect behavior for conventional channels. The three primary enhancements
it introduces are: (1) availability checking before connecting an incoming PSTN call to a
subscriber unit, (2) improved call progress signaling on outgoing calls, and (3) a cleaner
call teardown procedure using a structured termination message rather than only an analog
disconnect code. All procedures defined here are optional improvements layered on top of
the mandatory baseline from TIA/EIA-102.BADA.

WHAT IT SPECIFIES

Section 2 covers Subscriber Unit (SU) behavior. For outgoing calls (service requests), the
SU may optionally accept and display Acknowledgment, Deny, or Queued response signals
from the RFSS as call-progress indicators. If no such signals arrive, the SU must fall back
to the baseline behavior of simply playing received audio. For incoming calls, the SU may
optionally receive a Telephone Interconnect Answer Request from the RFSS, alert the user,
and then send a Telephone Interconnect Answer Proceed or Deny response to indicate
whether the user wishes to accept the call. This is the "availability check" mechanism —
analogous to a ring on a desk phone. For call termination, the SU may optionally send a
Cancel Service Request message to instruct the RFSS to disconnect the active call; SUs
that support this must also retain backward-compatible support for the legacy DTMF
disconnect code.

Section 3 covers RF Subsystem (RFSS) behavior. For outgoing service requests, the RFSS
may optionally send Acknowledge, Deny, or Queued responses back to the requesting SU,
and is recommended to relay call progress tones from the telephone network (with the
caveat that the P25 vocoder may distort or suppress DTMF tones). For incoming
availability checks, the RFSS transmits a Telephone Interconnect Answer Request to the
targeted SU and provides ring-back tone to the PSTN while awaiting the response. If the
SU answers Proceed, the RFSS connects the call and signals answer supervision to the
telephone network. If the SU answers Deny, the RFSS connects the call to a tone signal,
recorded announcement, operator position, or voicemail. If no response arrives within 1
second, the RFSS may abandon the attempt and route to alternate treatment. The RFSS
must be configurable to disable availability checks on a per-subscriber or per-system basis
to interoperate with SUs that do not support the feature. For call teardown, when the RFSS
disconnects the call it must send a call termination message per TIA-102.AABF (Link
Control Word Formats, clause 6.3.8 Call Termination/Cancellation) on the traffic or
conventional channel; an audible confirmation may precede the teardown on conventional
channels.

KEY MESSAGES AND SIGNALS

This addendum references message types by name rather than defining their wire formats —
the actual encoding is specified in:
  - TIA-102.AABF-A (Link Control Word Formats and Messages, Dec 2004): provides the
    LC TELE INT V CH USR word and the call termination LC word used in §3.4.1
  - TIA-102.AABG-1 (Conventional Control Messages Addendum 1 — Individual Telephone
    Calls, SP-3-3604-AD1): provides the Telephone Interconnect Answer Request/Response,
    Acknowledge/Deny/Queued response, and Cancel Service Request messages

MANDATORY VS OPTIONAL FEATURE TABLES (ANNEX A — NORMATIVE)

Annex A replaces Tables A.1 and A.2 in the base document TIA/EIA-102.BADA with updated
tables that incorporate conventional individual call features.

For Subscriber Equipment (Table A.1), notable distinctions between trunked/composite and
conventional:
  - Explicit dialing modes (Buffered/Live Key/List): Mandatory in trunked; conventional
    compliance subject to Note A.3.2
  - Voice Channel Grant acceptance: Mandatory in trunked; Not Applicable in conventional
  - Acknowledge/Deny/Queued response reception: Not Applicable / Mandatory in trunked;
    Optional in conventional (new capability added by this addendum)
  - Telephone Interconnect Answer Request/Response: Standard Option in trunked;
    Optional in conventional (new capability added by this addendum)
  - Cancel Service Request: Mandatory in trunked; Optional in conventional
  - Group Incoming Call: Standard Option in trunked; Mandatory in conventional
  - Hookflash Signaling: Standard Option in trunked; Mandatory in conventional

For RF Subsystem (Table A.2), notable distinctions:
  - Class of Service per-subscriber enabling: Standard Option in trunked; See Note 5
    in conventional
  - Toll restriction, speed dialing, outgoing/incoming only, hookflash: Standard Option
    in trunked; Mandatory in conventional
  - Voice Channel Grant (send): Mandatory in trunked; Not Applicable in conventional
  - Acknowledge response (send): Not Applicable in trunked; Optional in conventional
  - Answer Request/Deny/Queued (send): Standard Option / Mandatory in trunked;
    Optional in conventional (new capability from this addendum)
  - Cancel Service Request (accept): Mandatory in trunked; Optional in conventional
  - 2-wire loop start at Et point: Standard Option in trunked; Mandatory in conventional
  - Overdial generation: Standard Option in trunked; Mandatory in conventional
  - Individually targeted incoming calls: Mandatory in trunked; Optional in conventional
  - Group incoming calls: Standard Option in trunked; Mandatory in conventional

MESSAGE SEQUENCE (ANNEX B — INFORMATIVE)

Figure B.1 shows the complete message sequence for an incoming PSTN call on a
conventional system with availability checking enabled. The sequence is: PSTN CALL ALERT
arrives at RFSS → RFSS sends TELE_INT_ANS_REQ to SU while providing ring-back to PSTN →
SU user answers, SU sends TELE_INT_ANS_RSP (Proceed) → RFSS signals ANSWER supervision
to PSTN → bidirectional SPEECH established. When the SU user keys PTT, LC TELE INT V CH
USR link control words are exchanged, and speech flows in the outgoing direction.

RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER DOCUMENTS

This addendum sits directly on top of TIA/EIA-102.BADA and should be read in conjunction
with it. It explicitly extends the following clauses of the base document: 2.1.2.4
(SU service request outgoing), 2.4.2 (SU availability check), 2.6.1.2 (SU call
termination), 3.7 (RFSS service requests and availability check), 3.14.1 (RFSS call
termination by radio user), 3.14.2 (disconnect code), and 3.14.3 (RFSS-initiated call
teardown). The next complete revision of TIA/EIA-102.BADA was intended to incorporate the
content of this addendum directly. The message encoding for the new conventional control
messages is specified in TIA-102.AABG-1 (Conventional Control Messages Addendum 1).

LIMITATIONS AND NOTES

This addendum notes that not all specifications contained in the document were fully
operationally tested at time of publication. Call progress tones relayed from the PSTN
through the P25 vocoder may be distorted or blocked. Availability checking is an optional
feature, and system operators must configure the RFSS to skip availability checks for SUs
that do not support it, in order to maintain interoperability. The SU interoperability
requirement ensures that any telephone-interconnect-capable SU receiving incoming voice
will present it to the user regardless of whether an availability check preceded the call.
