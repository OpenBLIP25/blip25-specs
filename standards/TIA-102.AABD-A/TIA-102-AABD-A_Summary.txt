---

WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS

TIA-102.AABD-A-2 is the second addendum to TIA-102.AABD-A, "Project 25 Trunking
Procedures." It is a targeted, 20-page standards amendment with a single normative
scope: adding Conventional Fallback procedures to the trunking standard. The base
document defines how subscriber units (SUs) and RF subsystems (RFSSs) operate on
P25 trunked networks — channel acquisition, registration, call setup, and maintenance.
This addendum extends the base standard to define what happens when the trunking
control infrastructure fails and one or more repeaters must provide degraded-mode
conventional service to the coverage area.

---

WHY THIS DOCUMENT EXISTS

Trunked P25 systems depend on a functioning control channel and RFSS controller. When
that infrastructure fails — due to equipment failure, maintenance outage, or disaster
conditions — radios configured only for trunking lose their ability to communicate. Prior
to this addendum, the behavior of trunked radios in this scenario was unspecified.
Conventional Fallback is a graceful degradation mechanism: a repeater with sufficient
intelligence can detect the loss of trunking control and begin broadcasting in conventional
mode, allowing previously-trunked radios to fall back to basic repeater operation.
This addendum specifies the procedures precisely enough to ensure that SUs from
different manufacturers and FNEs from different vendors can interoperate in this
degraded mode.

---

WHAT IT DEFINES

The addendum adds or amends five technical areas:

1. CONTROL CHANNEL HUNTING (Section 5 amendments): Adds two new triggers to the
   existing control channel hunt state machine. A short hunt is now triggered when
   conventional fallback operation ends (so the SU can return to trunking). An extended
   hunt now escalates to a conventional fallback search when no control channel data
   is found. An updated Figure 5-1 replaces the original, adding a "SYSTEM ID
   CONVENTIONAL FALLBACK" state reached when the extended hunt exhausts without
   finding a valid control channel.

2. CONVENTIONAL FALLBACK OVERVIEW (Section 16.1): Conventional Fallback is optional
   and requires deliberate configuration of both the FNE repeaters and the SUs with
   matching Fallback Channel IDs. A repeater broadcasts the LC_CONV_FALLBACK Link
   Control Word (LCW) to signal its fallback state. The LCW includes up to six Fallback
   CH IDs; an SU selects a fallback channel by matching its configured CH IDs against
   those broadcast. Wildcard (CH ID = 0) allows SUs to find a fallback channel even
   without a specific pre-configured match, as a last resort.

3. FNE PROCEDURES (Sections 16.2–16.5): Defines what the repeater must do during
   fallback. During idle, the repeater continuously transmits (or intermittently, for power
   conservation), sending LC_CONV_FALLBACK at least every T_Conv_Fallback_LC (default
   10 seconds). During active calls, it repeats voice and data per TIA-102.BAAD-A and
   replaces LDU1 LCWs with LC_CONV_FALLBACK on the same timer. When trunking
   resumes, the repeater stops sending LC_CONV_FALLBACK and returns to its normal
   trunked role (control channel, traffic channel, packet data, or composite).

4. SU PROCEDURES (Sections 16.6–16.11): Defines the SU fallback state machine. After
   exhausting extended control channel hunt, the SU scans the short-hunt channel set
   (plus any SU-configured additions), waiting up to T_Conv_Fallback_LC per channel for
   an LC_CONV_FALLBACK. Matching on Fallback CH ID and NAC selects the channel.
   Wildcard channels are remembered and used only if no specific match is found.
   If no fallback channel is found, the SU re-runs the extended hunt, alternating
   indefinitely. The SU may exit fallback at any time to search for the control channel;
   it must exit after missing N_Conv_Fallback_LC (default 2) consecutive LC_CONV_FALLBACK
   messages or detecting a control channel on the current channel. On exit, the SU uses
   its remembered control channel parameters and follows the registration decision logic
   from the base standard.

5. OPERATIONAL PARAMETERS (Section 17): Adds two timers/counters to the parameter
   table: T_Conv_Fallback_LC (maximum interval between LC_CONV_FALLBACK transmissions,
   default 10 seconds) and N_Conv_Fallback_LC (number of missed LC_CONV_FALLBACK
   messages before exiting fallback, default 2). Also addresses talkgroup continuity
   (SU transmits with its last affiliated talkgroup unless specifically configured for
   fallback), audible indication (synced to the T flag in LC_CONV_FALLBACK), networked
   operation (the N flag signals simulcast/networked vs. single-site), and encryption
   (SU uses its configured fallback encryption parameters, recommended to match
   those used during trunked operation). Dynamic regroup state is cleared on fallback entry
   and should be re-established by the FNE when trunking resumes.

RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER DOCUMENTS

This addendum is one layer of a multi-document stack:

- TIA-102.AABD-A (December 2008): Base trunking procedures standard. Defines all normal
  trunked operation including control channel acquisition, registration, group/individual
  calls, site trunking, and the parameter table that this addendum extends.
- TIA-102.AABD-A-1 (April 2011): Addendum 1, which added multiple-channel and other
  enhancements (separate document in the same directory).
- TIA-102.AABD-A-2 (September 2012): This document — adds Conventional Fallback.
- TIA-102.AABD-B: A later complete revision of the trunking procedures, which
  incorporates the addenda into a unified document.
- TIA-102.BAAD-A (February 2010): Conventional Procedures standard. Referenced
  normatively — during fallback, the SU and repeater operate per BAAD-A rules for
  voice and data handling.
- TIA-102.AABF / AABG: Define the Link Control Word formats used on the traffic channel,
  including the LC_CONV_FALLBACK LCW format referenced throughout this addendum.

---

LIMITATIONS AND OPEN ISSUES

The document acknowledges several items outside its scope or left implementation-defined:
- The specific format and bit fields of LC_CONV_FALLBACK are defined in related LCW
  documents (not repeated here).
- Audible indication parameters (duration, frequency, waveform) are beyond scope.
- How the SU chooses among multiple wildcard channels is manufacturer-defined.
- The T_Conv_Fallback_LC minimum value is unspecified; FNEs may use values shorter
  than the 10s default to trade faster SU identification against other operational needs.
- Conformance tests for the Conventional Fallback procedures had not been developed
  at time of publication.

---

OPEN-SOURCE AND IMPLEMENTATION RELEVANCE

The LC_CONV_FALLBACK detection and fallback channel scan logic affects SDR-based P25
receivers (SDRTrunk, OP25) that monitor trunked systems. A correctly implemented
monitor should be able to follow a site into fallback mode by detecting the transition
from control channel PDUs to LC_CONV_FALLBACK in LDU1 or extended terminators.
The parameters T_Conv_Fallback_LC and N_Conv_Fallback_LC inform the timing for any
state machine implementation of this feature.
