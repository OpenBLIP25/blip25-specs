DOCUMENT PURPOSE AND SCOPE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TIA-102.BCAF defines 29 Conformance Profiles (CPs) for the two-slot TDMA trunked
voice air interface, collectively called "Trunked TDMA Voice Channel Conformance
Profiles." Each CP specifies reference signaling sequences — exact MAC PDU types,
DUID values, field-level bit parameters, and pre/post-conditions — that an RFSS Site
and/or Subscriber Unit (SU) must produce to be considered conformant.

The document supplements interoperability testing ([CABC]) and is distinct from the
TDMA CAI conformance specification ([BCAD]). It supports TIA-102.BBAC and the
two-slot TDMA media access control layer. Tests operate at the Um2 (TDMA voice
channel) interface without requiring internal access to equipment hardware or software.

Conformance is assessed separately for SU and RFSS Site roles. Pass/Fail is binary:
(1) the system under test generates the correct messages in the correct sequence with
the correct field values as defined in each Signaling Parameters section, and (2) all
post-conditions listed for the CP are satisfied.

CHANNEL STRUCTURE AND KEY TERMINOLOGY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The TDMA traffic channel carries two types of burst:
- FACCH (Fast Access Control Channel): Control bursts carrying MAC PDUs
- SACCH (Slow Access Control Channel): Occasional control bursts interleaved with voice

SACCH slots have four designations:
- IT (Inbound Talker): Uplink SACCH from the transmitting SU
- IL (Inbound Listener): Uplink SACCH from a non-transmitting SU — SUs SHALL NOT
  transmit in IL SACCH slots during active transmission
- OT (Outbound Talker): RFSS downlink SACCH during talker's transmission
- OL (Outbound Listener): RFSS downlink SACCH to listening SUs

Voice superframe: 4Va → 4Vb → 4Vc → 4Vd → 2V (repeating, 5 bursts)
ESS-A distribution per superframe: 4Va=ALGID/KEYID; 4Vb=MI[71:48]; 4Vc=MI[47:24]; 4Vd=MI[23:0]
ESS-B (in 2V burst): FEC data

DUID values: %0000=4V, %0011=SACCH scrambled, %0110=2V, %1001=FACCH scrambled,
%1100=SACCH unscrambled, %1111=FACCH unscrambled

MAC PDU opcodes: MAC_ACTIVE=%100, MAC_PTT=%001, MAC_END_PTT=%010, MAC_IDLE=%011

MAC_RELEASE MCO=%110001: forces SU to release channel
  C/A=%0 = Leave voice channel (preemption — SU returns to CC)
  C/A=%1 = Stay on voice channel but stop transmitting (forced dekey)

CONFORMANCE PROFILE INVENTORY (29 CPs)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROUP VOICE CALL PROFILES (CPs 1–13):

CP 1 — TDMA Unit to Group Voice Call Procedure (Group Call Start)
  SU initiates a trunked group call. Sends GRP_V_REQ on CC; RFSS responds with
  GRP_V_CH_GRANT (implicit TSBK or explicit PDU). SU sends MAC_PTT 1 then
  MAC_PTT 0 in consecutive FACCHs, then transmits repeating superframe (4Va/4Vb/
  4Vc/4Vd/2V). RFSS sources SU audio outbound. SU sends MAC_ACTIVE in every IT
  SACCH; must not transmit in IL SACCH.

CP 2 — TDMA Unit to Group Voice Call Transmission Terminate Procedure
  SU ends its transmission by sending ≥2 MAC_END_PTT PDUs in consecutive FACCHs.
  Hangtime behavior is manufacturer-defined; RFSS may send MAC_HANGTIME or
  MAC_ACTIVE during hangtime period.

CP 3 — TDMA Unit to Group Voice Call Termination Procedure
  Call is torn down. RFSS sends ≥2 MAC_END_PTT PDUs; SUs leave traffic channel and
  return to control channel. RFSS either shuts down transmitter or sends MAC_IDLE.

CP 4 — TDMA Unit to Group Call Continuation Procedure
  Alternate A (Control Channel, R-bit=%1): SU returns to CC during hangtime, sends
  new GRP_V_REQ, receives GRP_V_CH_GRANT, re-establishes as talker.
  Alternate B (Voice Channel, R-bit=%0): SU sends MAC_PTT 1 directly on voice channel
  without returning to CC.

CP 5 — TDMA Unit to Group Call SU Interrupt by a Console Procedure
  Console takes over outbound audio while SU continues transmitting inbound. RFSS
  sends MAC_PTT from Console in outbound FACCHs; sources Console audio outbound.
  SU continues inbound transmission. Includes SU Resumption sub-procedure after
  Console releases the channel.

CP 6 — TDMA Unit to Group Call Audio Preemption Forced SU Dekey Procedure
  Console demands the channel. RFSS sends MAC_RELEASE (MCO=%110001, C/A=%1, stay
  on voice channel) to force SU to stop transmitting. SU sends ≥2 MAC_END_PTT; RFSS
  then sources Console audio outbound.

CP 7 — TDMA Call Preemption Procedure (4 sub-procedures):
  A) SU Polite Preemption via CC: Emergency SU sends GRP_V_REQ; RFSS releases
     current talker via MAC_RELEASE (C/A=%0, leave channel), then grants new talker.
  B) SU Impolite Preemption via CC: RFSS releases current talker and simultaneously
     grants interrupting SU — current talker may receive grant before release.
  C) Console Polite Preemption: RFSS sends MAC_RELEASE (C/A=%0) to current talker;
     after SU leaves, sources Console audio.
  D) Console Impolite Preemption: RFSS releases current talker and simultaneously
     starts Console audio.

CP 8 — TDMA Emergency Group Call Procedure
  References CPs 6–11 executed with E-bit=1 in Service Options. No new procedures.

CP 9 — TDMA Encrypted Group Call Procedure
  References CPs 6–12 executed with P-bit=1, ALGID/KID/MI set to traffic values.

CP 10 — TDMA Announcement Group Call Procedure
  References CPs 6–12. No new procedures.

CP 11 — TDMA Broadcast Group Call Procedure
  Always transmission trunked. References CPs 6 and 8. No new procedures.

CP 12 — TDMA System Call Procedure
  Console-originated, system-wide call using WGID=$FFFF. Always transmission trunked.
  RFSS sends MAC_PTT with Group Address=WGID_sys_wide=$FFFF in Group Voice Channel
  User MAC message. Terminates per CP 3.

CP 13 — TDMA Radio Unit Monitor Procedure — Group
  References CPs 6 and 7. CC procedures in [CAEC]/[CAED]. No new TDMA procedures.

UNIT TO UNIT VOICE CALL PROFILES (CPs 14–20):

CP 14 — TDMA Unit to Unit Call Start Procedure
  Mirrors CP 1 but uses UU_V_REQ/UU_V_CH_GRANT on CC. Voice channel uses Unit to
  Unit Voice Channel User MAC Message (MCO=%000010, Target=WUID_dest).

CP 15 — TDMA Unit to Unit Call Transmission Termination Procedure
  Mirrors CP 2 for Unit to Unit context.

CP 16 — TDMA Unit to Unit Call Continuation Procedure
  Mirrors CP 4 (CC and VC alternates) for Unit to Unit context using UU_V_REQ.

CP 17 — TDMA Unit to Unit Call Termination Procedure
  SU Termination: SU sends CAN_SRV_REQ on CC; RFSS sends MAC_END_PTT; SUs leave.
  Site Termination: RFSS directly sends MAC_END_PTT; SUs leave.

CP 18 — TDMA Emergency Unit to Unit Call Procedure
  References CPs 14–17 with E-bit=1.

CP 19 — TDMA Encrypted Unit to Unit Call Procedure
  References CPs 14–17 with P-bit=1.

CP 20 — TDMA Radio Unit Monitor Procedure — Individual
  References CPs 14 and 17. CC procedures in [CAEC]/[CAED].

TELEPHONE INTERCONNECT PROFILES (CPs 21–29):

CP 21 — TDMA Unit to PSTN and PSTN to Unit Call Start Procedure
  SU-initiated interconnect call. Telephone Interconnect Voice Channel User MAC Message
  (MCO=%000011) used. RFSS routes PSTN audio outbound to SU after call establishment.

CP 22 — TDMA Unit to PSTN and PSTN to Unit Call SU Transmission Procedure
  SU talks during interconnect call; both PSTN and Destination SUs hear SU. SU sends
  GRP_V_REQ (or equivalent) on CC, receives grant, then transmits inbound superframe.

CP 23 — TDMA Unit to PSTN and PSTN to Unit Call SU Transmission Termination Procedure
  SU sends ≥2 MAC_END_PTT; RFSS resumes PSTN audio outbound.

CP 24 — TDMA Unit to PSTN and PSTN to Unit Call Termination Procedure
  SU sends CAN_SRV_REQ (Service Type=Tele_Int_Req); RFSS sends MAC_END_PTT; call ends.

CP 25 — TDMA PSTN to Group Call Start Procedure
  PSTN user initiates group call. RFSS sources PSTN audio outbound; Destination SUs
  play PSTN audio. Group Voice Channel User MAC Message with Source=WUID_pstn.

CP 26 — TDMA PSTN to Group Call SU Transmission Procedure (2 alternates)
  Alt A (CC Request): SU sends GRP_V_REQ on CC; receives grant; transmits inbound
    while RFSS simultaneously continues PSTN audio stream outbound (dual-stream).
  Alt B (VC Request): SU sends Group Voice Service Req MAC message (B1=%0, B2=%1,
    MCO=%000001) in IL SACCH; RFSS grants via OL SACCH MAC message; then same flow.
  Both alts produce concurrent SU inbound superframe and RFSS outbound PSTN superframe.

CP 27 — TDMA PSTN to Group Call SU Transmission Termination Procedure
  SU sends ≥2 MAC_END_PTT; RFSS resumes PSTN audio. In MAC_END_PTT, Group Address=0
  (not group address) when SU terminates within a PSTN to Group call.

CP 28 — TDMA PSTN to Group Call Termination Procedure
  PSTN terminates call. RFSS sends ≥2 MAC_END_PTT; SUs leave traffic channel.
  Key: MAC_END_PTT Source Address=$FFFFFF indicates PSTN-sourced termination.

CP 29 — TDMA Encrypted Telephone Interconnect Call Procedures
  References CPs 21–28 with P-bit=1. No new procedures.

PROCEDURAL CONVENTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Flow designators in procedure figures:
- Fx (solid line): Required flows — UUT must produce these in sequence
- FxA (solid line, alternate): Location-dependent; either FxA or the base Fx is
  required depending on SACCH timing alignment
- Fx* or FxA* (dashed line): Optional flows; if present, must conform to specified
  signaling parameters

MAC_PTT offset field encodes position of first 4V burst relative to current PDU:
  %000=next non-SACCH burst, %001=2nd, %010=3rd, %011=4th, %100=5th,
  %110=Random Access SACCH (inbound only), %111=No voice framing

Control Channel grant forms:
- Implicit: TSBK (DUID=%0111) with channel identifier and channel number
- Explicit: Alternative Multiblock Trunking Control PDU (DUID=%1100, Format=%10111,
  SAP=$3D) with separate TX/RX channel identifier and number

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

- Phase 3 (Implementation Spec): NOT WARRANTED for this document type. The CPs
  define test harness signaling sequences — not implementation algorithms. An
  implementer writing a P25 Phase 2 stack should use TIA-102.BBAC and TIA-102.AABD
  as the normative behavioral references; this document validates that the resulting
  implementation produces correct over-the-air signals.

- The document does NOT cover non-TDMA-specific control channel procedures (CC
  registration, affiliation, etc.) — those are in [CAEC] and [CAED].

- Some CP items are conditional on FNE/SU capability (e.g., telephone interconnect
  calls, broadcast calls) and are explicitly noted "Execute this CP only if..."

- Encryption parameters (ALGID, KID, MI) always appear in ESS on the voice channel
  regardless of whether encryption is active. For unencrypted calls: ALGID=$80,
  KID=0, MI=0. For encrypted calls: values per [BAAC].
