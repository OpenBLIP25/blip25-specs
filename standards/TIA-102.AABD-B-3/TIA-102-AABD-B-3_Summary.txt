TIA-102.AABD-B-3: Project 25 Trunking Procedures Addendum — Remotely Activated Emergency
Summary
Published: February 2025 | Addendum to TIA-102.AABD-B | TIA TR-8.10 Air Interface Subcommittee

OVERVIEW

TIA-102.AABD-B-3 is a short (six-page normative content) addendum to the Project 25 Trunking Procedures standard. It defines the air-interface procedures for a new P25 trunking service called Remotely Activated Emergency (RAE). The RAE service enables a sufficiently privileged user — operating either from a dispatch console or a mobile subscriber unit — to remotely trigger an emergency alarm on another subscriber unit (SU) whose user is unable to activate it themselves. The addendum inserts new section 11.2.4 into TIA-102.AABD-B and adds a call-sequence diagram (Figure 18-12) to Annex D of that document. It introduces no new air-interface message formats; all PDU definitions referenced here (RAE_REQ, RAE_CMD, ACK_RSP_FNE, ACK_RSP_U, DENY_RSP, EMRG_ALM_REQ) are defined in the parent TIA-102.AABD-B and its companion Control Channel Messages specification referenced as [1].

PURPOSE AND MOTIVATION

The RAE service addresses a recognized gap in P25 emergency alerting: situations where a field officer or first responder is incapacitated, coerced, or otherwise unable to press the emergency button on their radio. A dispatch supervisor or a partner unit can remotely trigger the emergency alarm on the target radio over the existing trunked air interface, causing it to transmit an EMRG_ALM_REQ with a special RAE bit set that identifies the alarm as remotely initiated. This flag allows receiving systems to distinguish remotely activated alarms from user-initiated alarms, which can be important for dispatch workflows and logging.

KEY ENTITIES AND ROLES

Three logical entities participate in the RAE procedure:

1. Initiating SU (SU1): The subscriber unit or dispatch console that sends the RAE_REQ to its serving RFSS. Must be "sufficiently privileged" — the RFSS enforces authorization.

2. RFSS (RF Subsystem): The trunked network infrastructure that accepts the RAE_REQ, validates authorization, routes the RAE_CMD to the target SU's RFSS (possibly via ISSI), manages timers, and relays acknowledgments back to the initiating SU.

3. Target SU (SU2): The subscriber unit whose WUID appears in the RAE_CMD. Upon receipt, it autonomously initiates the emergency alarm procedure and sends EMRG_ALM_REQ with the RAE bit set, then responds with ACK_RSP_U.

PROCEDURE FLOW

The RAE procedure operates as follows:

Step 1 — Initiation: The initiating user selects a target SU. If the initiating user is at a mobile SU, that SU sends a RAE_REQ over the control channel to its RFSS (per the random-access protocol of AABD section 4), carrying both the source and destination SU addresses. If the initiating user is resident in the RFSS (e.g., a wireline dispatch console), the RAE_REQ is generated internally in the RFSS and section 11.2.4.2 is bypassed.

Step 2 — RFSS Processing: The RFSS may respond to a RAE_REQ with any combination of: (a) DENY_RSP if the request is not authorized; (b) ACK_RSP_FNE sourced by the FNE to acknowledge receipt without final disposition; (c) RAE_CMD sent to the target SU; or (d) ACK_RSP_FNE sourced by the target SU (indicating the target has accepted the command). If the target is co-located on the same RFSS, the RFSS sends the RAE_CMD directly and starts a local timer. If the target is at a different RFSS, the initiator RFSS forwards the request via the ISSI, and both RFSSs independently run timers: the initiator RFSS waiting on the ISSI return, the target RFSS waiting for ACK_RSP_U from the target SU.

Step 3 — Initiating SU Behavior: After sending RAE_REQ (with retries per AABD section 4.1.3), the initiating SU processes responses as follows: DENY_RSP causes a return to idle with optional user notification; an ACK_RSP_FNE from the target unit causes a return to idle with success indication; receipt of ACK_RSP_FNE from the FNE or an overheard RAE_CMD causes the initiating SU to end its retry loop and start timer T_ACK. If T_ACK expires before an ACK_RSP_FNE sourced from the target SU is received, the operation is declared failed.

Step 4 — Target SU Behavior: On receiving a RAE_CMD whose target address matches its own WUID, the target SU must either: (a) respond with ACK_RSP_U to the initiating SU and autonomously initiate its locally defined emergency alarm procedure (EMRG_ALM_REQ with RAE bit set, per section 11.2.1), or (b) ignore the message if the SU does not support this service. Ignoring is explicitly permitted for backward-compatible devices.

Step 5 — RFSS Cleanup: The target RFSS cancels its timer on receipt of ACK_RSP_U and relays ACK_RSP_FNE (sourced as the target SU) to the initiating SU, per the signaling reliability rule of section 3.6. On timer expiry without ACK_RSP_U: if both initiator and target are on the same RFSS, it simply ends processing; if on different RFSSs, the target RFSS sends an unsuccessful forwarding indication via ISSI (defined in TIA-102.BACD-B) and ends processing, while the initiator RFSS ends processing upon receiving that indication or its own timer expiry.

RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER DOCUMENTS

This addendum is narrowly scoped and depends heavily on the parent TIA-102.AABD-B for all trunking procedure context (random-access protocols, signaling reliability rules, timer definitions, and message formats). The EMRG_ALM_REQ procedure it invokes at the target SU is defined in TIA-102.AABD-B section 11.2.1. The ISSI unsuccessful forwarding indication that bounds RFSS failure handling is defined in TIA-102.BACD-B (ISSI standard). The RAE_REQ and RAE_CMD message format and opcode definitions are in the Control Channel Messages specification (referenced as [1] in the parent document). The addendum will be folded into the next full revision of TIA-102.AABD-B.

LIMITATIONS AND SCOPE NOTES

The document explicitly notes: (a) the unsuccessful forwarding indication over the ISSI is out of scope here and deferred to TIA-102.BACD-B; (b) no timer values for T_ACK or RFSS timers are defined within this addendum — implementers must consult TIA-102.AABD-B for those parameters; (c) the RAE service is optional — target SUs that do not implement it are permitted to silently ignore RAE_CMD. No conformance requirements or test procedures are defined in this addendum.
