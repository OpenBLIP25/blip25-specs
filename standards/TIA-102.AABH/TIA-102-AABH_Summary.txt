TIA-102.AABH: Project 25 Dynamic Regrouping Messages and Procedures
Summary (~1,000 words)
Published: November 2014 | Committee: TIA TR-8.10

WHAT THIS DOCUMENT SPECIFIES

This document specifies the over-the-air messaging and subscriber unit (SU) / Radio Frequency
Sub-System (RFSS) behavioral procedures for Dynamic Regrouping operations in a P25 trunked
radio system. Dynamic Regrouping encompasses two related features: Group Regrouping and
Individual Regrouping.

Group Regrouping allows a dispatch console or system administrator to dynamically merge
multiple talkgroups into a temporary composite talkgroup called a "supergroup," identified by a
Supergroup Working Group ID (SP-WGID). The SP-WGID is drawn from a pool of addresses
reserved specifically for supergroups and not used for normal talkgroup operation, so SUs can
distinguish supergroup assignments from ordinary channel grants. Group Regrouping is used to
conserve RF voice channels—rather than granting separate channels to each constituent
talkgroup, the RFSS can place all members on one channel with a single grant. Group
Regrouping supports two call modes: a two-way patch (bidirectional voice on the supergroup)
and a one-way simulselect (RFSS/console transmits outbound to the supergroup; SUs receive
on the supergroup but transmit on their original WGID).

Individual Regrouping is a MFID90-only feature that commands a specific SU to affiliate with a
new dynamically assigned talkgroup ("dynamic group"), effectively replacing the SU's currently
selected talkgroup for the duration of the regrouped state.

WHY IT EXISTS

Before this document was published, two incompatible proprietary regrouping methods had
already been deployed in the field by different P25 infrastructure vendors: the "MFIDA4 Explicit
Encryption" method (using Manufacturer ID $A4) and the "MFID90 Airlink Efficient" method
(using Manufacturer ID $90, originally Motorola). Because both methods were widely fielded, the
TIA TR-8.10 committee elected to formally standardize both rather than deprecate either. An
RFSS may implement one or both methods; however, subscriber manufacturers are required to
implement both methods to ensure interoperability across different infrastructure vendors.

KEY PARAMETERS AND REQUIREMENTS

Group Regrouping Timer (T_grp_rgrp): Default 20 seconds (minimum 20 s, maximum
unspecified). SUs leave the Group Regrouped state if they do not receive a refreshing Group
Regroup command before timer expiration. The timer runs only while the SU monitors the control
channel and is paused while on a traffic channel.

Supergroup Sequence Number (SSN): A unique non-zero 5-bit value embedded in the
GRG_Options field of MFIDA4 commands. It prevents SUs from remaining in the regrouped
state when an SP-WGID is reused for a new supergroup after teardown.

Encryption handling differs by method. In MFIDA4 (Explicit Encryption), the Algorithm ID and
Key ID are carried directly in the GRG_EXENC_CMD message for patch operations (T=0). For
simulselect (T=1), the Key ID and Algorithm ID are set to 0. In MFID90 (Airlink Efficient),
encryption is pre-configured in both infrastructure and subscriber units; the regrouping messages
carry only a P-bit indicating whether the channel grant is for an encrypted or clear transmission.

MFIDA4 MESSAGES DEFINED

- GRG_EXENC_CMD (OSP, opcode %110000, MFID $A4): The sole MFIDA4-specific message.
  Carries GRG_Options byte (Type/Group/Activate flags + 5-bit SSN), SP-WGID, Key ID,
  Algorithm ID, and Regroup Target (WGID or WUID). Sent continuously for all active
  supergroups as part of normal control channel background scheduling.
- Standard ISPs and OSPs from TIA-102.AABC (GRP_V_REQ, GRP_V_CH_GRANT,
  GRP_V_CH_GRANT_UPDT, etc.) are reused for voice call setup; no dedicated MFIDA4 ISPs
  are defined in this document.
- Standard Link Control words from TIA-102.AABF and MAC messages from TIA-102.BBAC are
  reused on FDMA and TDMA traffic channels respectively.

MFID90 MESSAGES DEFINED

Trunking Control Channel (TSBK format):
- MOT_GRG_V_REQ (ISP, %000000): SU requests supergroup channel grant.
- MOT_EXT_FNCT_RSP (ISP, %000001): SU sends IR Command ACK or IR Cancel ACK.
- MOT_GRG_ADD_CMD (OSP, %000000): RFSS programs up to 3 WGIDs into supergroup.
- MOT_GRG_DEL_CMD (OSP, %000001): RFSS removes up to 3 WGIDs from supergroup.
- MOT_GRG_CN_GRANT (OSP, %000010): Initial channel grant for supergroup.
- MOT_GRG_CN_GRANT_EXP (OSP, %000010, alternate format): Explicit channel grant with
  separate transmit and receive channel identifiers for inter-site cases.
- MOT_GRG_CN_GRANT_UPDT (OSP, %000011): Channel update carrying two simultaneous
  supergroup channel assignments (A and B).
- MOT_EXT_FNCT_CMD (OSP, %000100): RFSS sends IR Command or IR Cancel to a target SU.
- MOT_QUE_RSP (OSP, %000110): Proprietary queued response for supergroup requests.
- MOT_DENY_RSP (OSP, %000111): Proprietary deny response; reason codes include
  "secure request on clear supergroup" ($00) and "clear request on secure supergroup" ($01).
- MOT_ACK_RSP_FNE (OSP, %001000): FNE acknowledgment of SU service request.

Link Control Words (FDMA traffic channel):
- LC_MOT_GRG_V_CH_USR (LCO %000000): Identifies transmitting unit and SP-WGID on traffic
  channel. Used both inbound and outbound. Carries P-bit (encryption state), SP-WGID,
  source WUID, and S-bit (explicit source ID required flag).
- LC_MOT_GRG_V_CH_UPDT (LCO %000001): Outbound only; notifies SUs of a supergroup
  call occurring on another channel.

TDMA MAC Messages (Two-Slot TDMA traffic channel):
- MAC_MOT_GRG_V_CH_USR (MCO %000000, 8 bytes): Abbreviated format for home system.
- MAC_MOT_GRG_V_CH_USR_EXT (MCO %100000, 16 bytes): Extended format (roaming); adds
  WACN ID, System ID, and Source ID for full SUID identification.
- MAC_MOT_GRG_V_CH_UPDT (MCO %000011, 7 bytes): Channel update for TDMA.
- MAC_MOT_EXT_FNCT_CMD (MCO %000100, 11 bytes): IR Command/Cancel on TDMA.

SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS

- This document covers FDMA (Um) and Two-Slot TDMA (Um2) Common Air Interfaces.
- Individual Regrouping is defined only for MFID90; MFIDA4 does not include an individual
  regrouping procedure.
- Simulselect is defined only for MFIDA4; MFID90 does not specify simulselect behavior.
- Announcement Groups shall not be regrouped under MFID90. Under MFIDA4, announcement
  group behavior is specified (the SU enters the Group Regrouped state when its AGID is
  included in a supergroup).
- Emergency operation differs between methods: MFIDA4 processes emergencies on the
  SP-WGID; MFID90 requires SUs to leave the Group Regrouped state and send a standard
  GRP_V_REQ on the emergency working group.
- The criteria for initiating or canceling a Group Regroup (console or administrator actions,
  inter-RFSS coordination) are outside the scope of this document.
