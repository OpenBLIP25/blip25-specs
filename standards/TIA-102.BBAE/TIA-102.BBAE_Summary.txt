SUMMARY: TIA-102.BBAE — Project 25 Two-Slot TDMA MAC Layer Procedures
Published: November 2019
Standard Body: Telecommunications Industry Association (TIA)
Subcommittee: TR-8.12 (Two-Slot TDMA)

WHAT THIS DOCUMENT SPECIFIES

This document defines the Media Access Control (MAC) layer procedures for the
Project 25 (P25) two-slot Time Division Multiple Access (TDMA) Common Air
Interface (CAI). It specifies how subscriber units (SUs) and fixed network equipment
(FNE) coordinate voice calls, control channel signaling, and channel management
on the Um2 air interface — the TDMA variant of P25's over-the-air protocol. The
air interface operates within a 12.5 kHz bandwidth physical radio channel using two
TDMA timeslots, effectively doubling spectral efficiency compared to the legacy
FDMA approach.

WHY IT EXISTS

P25 Phase II introduced two-slot TDMA as a spectrally efficient alternative to Phase
I's single-channel FDMA operation. While companion documents (TIA-102.BBAC and
TIA-102.BBAD) define the MAC layer specification and message formats respectively,
this document was created to consolidate the procedural logic — the step-by-step
behavioral rules that govern how equipment actually operates on the air interface.
It merges Voice Channel procedures previously split across TIA-102.BBAC section 7,
its BBAC-1 addendum, and Control Channel procedures from TIA-102.BBAD section 5
into a single unified reference. The P25 Steering Committee adopted it on
October 10, 2019.

SCOPE AND STRUCTURE

The document covers four categories of procedures:

General Procedures (Section 2) address unassigned logical channel handling,
synchronization, scrambling, and power control. These apply to all logical channel
types.

Control Channel (CCH) Procedures (Section 3) specify how TDMA Logical Control
Channels (LCCHs) are designated, acquired, and transitioned. This includes single
LCCH, dual LCCH, and composite (no dedicated LCCH) topologies, as well as how
SUs discover and use both current-site and adjacent-site control channels.

Voice Channel (VCH) Procedures (Section 4) form the bulk of the document,
covering the complete lifecycle of a trunked voice call: channel assignment and call
setup, traffic mode operation with ongoing talker/listener SACCH signaling,
call termination (both transmission-trunked and message-trunked), hangtime call
continuation, and preemption (audio takeover and call takeover).

Data Channel (DCH) Procedures (Section 5) are acknowledged as anticipated but
left undefined at the time of publication.

KEY TECHNICAL DETAILS

Scrambling: A 44-bit Linear Feedback Shift Register (LFSR) generates a per-
superframe scrambling sequence seeded from the WACN ID, System ID, and Color
Code. The generator polynomial is G(x) = x^44 + x^40 + x^35 + x^29 + x^24 +
x^10 + 1. The seed for inbound signals is derived by advancing the outbound seed
by 2^43 shift cycles using a precomputed 44x44 GF(2) matrix. Scrambling rejects
co-channel interference from adjacent TDMA systems. Specific burst types have
defined scrambling exceptions: MAC_END_PTT bursts and Network Status Broadcast
bursts are never scrambled, ensuring that Color Code identification and
descrambling parameters remain accessible.

Synchronization: Three levels are defined — symbol synchronization (detecting
sync sequences in ISCH or FACCH bursts), VCH synchronization (determining LCH
numbering, superframe/ultraframe framing, and voice burst sequencing), and CCH-
to-VCH synchronization (using Synchronization Broadcast messages to carry timing
alignment from the control channel to traffic channels). Transmitting SUs maintain
sync by monitoring the S-ISCH in SACCH slots 10/11.

Power Control: Open-loop (SU self-adjusts based on received signal) and
closed-loop (FNE sends Power Control Signal Quality MAC messages with BER
and power level data; SU adjusts accordingly) methods are defined for the
inbound TCH path only. No adaptive power control is used on the CCH or
outbound path.

Voice Call Lifecycle: Call setup requires the talker SU to send two MAC_PTT PDUs
on the FACCH after synchronizing. The FNE repeats these outbound. Voice framing
follows a 4V-4V-4V-4V-2V burst sequence within each superframe. During traffic
mode, the FNE uses the SACCH ultraframe schedule to send voice channel user
(VCU) messages identifying the talker to listeners, and to confirm the talker's
right to transmit. Call termination requires at least two MAC_END_PTT PDUs.
Message trunking supports hangtime continuation via either CCH (SU returns to
control channel to request) or VCH (SU transmits MAC_PTTs directly on the
voice channel).

Preemption: Two types are defined. Audio preemption (C/A=1 in MAC_Release)
replaces the current talker's audio with a higher-priority source while keeping the
call active — this can be forced (U/F=1, SU must stop) or unforced (U/F=0, SU
may continue). Call preemption (C/A=0) terminates the current call entirely to
free the channel for a different call, with polite (MAC_END_PTTs sent first) and
impolite (immediate transition) variants.

LCH Transitions: The system supports dynamic reconfiguration between dual LCCH,
single LCCH, and composite (no LCCH) topologies. Transitions are signaled via the
I-ISCH LCH Type field and are constrained to specific timeslot positions (0/4/8
for LCH 0; 1/5/9 for LCH 1).

CCCH Access Parameters: TDMA LCCH access timing differs from FDMA: T_LCCH =
30ms x N_slot (where N_slot=1 for dual LCCH, 2 for single LCCH). The T_ctrl
default is 240ms (vs. FDMA's different value), with a 120ms minimum.

INFORMATIVE ANNEXES

Annex A provides LCCH call control timing diagrams for single LCCH/same channel,
single LCCH/different channel, and dual LCCH configurations. Annex B shows PTT
initiation timing for all five possible inbound FACCH positions and call
termination. Annex C provides eight complete call flow examples including
unsynchronized and synchronized CCH entry, transmission and message trunked
unkey scenarios, call continuation via both TCH and CCH methods, and both
unforced and forced console-initiated preemption.

LIMITATIONS

The document explicitly leaves several items undefined or deferred to local
policy: the closed-loop power control algorithm, the method for selecting among
competing audio sources or preemption requestors at multi-site systems, user
notification mechanisms for preemption, and the total number of MAC_END_PTT
PDUs beyond the two-minimum requirement. The DCH procedures are entirely
undefined. The document notes that some specifications had not been fully
operationally tested at the time of publication.
