TIA-102.CAAB-E: Land Mobile Radio Transceiver Performance Recommendations,
Digital Radio Technology, C4FM/CQPSK Modulation

Document: TIA-102.CAAB-E (Revision of TIA-102.CAAB-D)
Published: September 2021
Status: Active (as of 2026)
Subcommittee: TR-8.1 Equipment Measurement Procedures
Classification: MEASUREMENT

==========================================================================
OVERVIEW
==========================================================================

This document establishes the pass/fail performance limit values for 12.5 kHz
channelized digitally modulated land mobile radio transceivers operating at
frequencies up to 1 GHz. It covers equipment using Compatible 4-Level
Frequency Modulation (C4FM), Compatible Differential Offset Quadrature Phase
Shift Keying (CQPSK), and standard simulcast modulations as defined in the
P25 physical layer standard (TIA-102.BAAA-B).

The companion document, TIA-102.CAAA-F, defines HOW to make the measurements;
this document defines WHAT the limits are. Together they form the complete
P25 transceiver qualification framework. Standards in this document reference
FCC Rules (47 CFR) as mandatory requirements, and NTIA Red Book values as
informative recommendations.

Two performance classes are defined: Class A (more stringent, for demanding
public safety applications) and Class B (comparable to 12.5 kHz analog FM
performance per TIA-603-E, for general applications). The document applies
to mobile, portable, and base station equipment. It explicitly addresses
public safety and non-public safety users equally.

==========================================================================
WHAT THE DOCUMENT SPECIFIES
==========================================================================

RECEIVER PERFORMANCE LIMITS (Section 3.1)

Radiated spurious field strength limits follow FCC Part 15 rules: 100 µV/m
at 30-88 MHz through 500 µV/m above 960 MHz, measured at 3 meters.
Conducted spurious output must not exceed -57 dBm at the antenna terminals.

Reference sensitivity (the weakest signal a receiver can decode): -116 dBm
(Class A) and -113 dBm (Class B), applied equally to mobile, portable, and
base station equipment. Faded reference sensitivity (under simulated
multipath): -108 dBm (Class A) and -105 dBm (Class B).

Signal delay spread capability sets the multipath tolerance: C4FM receivers
must handle 50 µs delay spread; standard simulcast receivers must handle 80 µs.

Adjacent channel rejection: 60 dB (Class A) and 50-60 dB (Class B) depending
on form factor. Offset digital adjacent channel rejection: 47 dB (Class A),
37 dB (Class B). Co-channel rejection: 9 dB maximum (applies to all classes).

Spurious response rejection: 80-90 dB (Class A) and 60-70 dB (Class B)
depending on form factor. Intermodulation rejection: 70-80 dB (Class A) and
50-70 dB (Class B).

Bit error rate floor: maximum 0.01% (no class distinction). Late entry
unsquelch delay: 125 ms (no talk group/encryption) or 370 ms (with talk group).
Receiver throughput delay for voice in conventional non-trunked: 125 ms max.

Blocking rejection (added in CAAB-D, 2013): Class A 80-90 dB; Class B 70-80 dB.

Broadband Strong Signal Intermodulation Rejection (SSIR) — NEW in CAAB-E (2021):
Power-level-dependent limits for 800 MHz, 700 MHz Band 14, 700 MHz Band 13,
and 900 MHz frequency ranges. At -40 dBm unwanted signal, Class A must
achieve 65-70 dB SSIR. At -10 dBm, limits relax significantly (30-55 dB)
reflecting reduced receiver linearity at high input levels.

TRANSMITTER PERFORMANCE LIMITS (Section 3.2)

RF output power: must meet or exceed manufacturer's rating; must not exceed
the rating by more than 20%. Airborne public safety radios: ≤10 watts.
700 MHz band mobiles/controls: ≤30 watts; portables: ≤3 watts.

Operating frequency accuracy: 5.0 PPM (mobile/portable, below 100 MHz) down
to 0.1 PPM (base station, 700 MHz band). 700 MHz mobiles with AFC locked to
base station must meet 0.4 PPM.

Modulation emission spectrum: FCC mandatory limits per §90.210(d) with a
breakpoint at 5.625 kHz (slope of 7.27 dB/kHz) and a floor of
50+10log10(RFOP) dB or 70 dB (whichever is less) beyond 12.5 kHz.

Frequency deviation for C4FM: High level 2544-3111 Hz, Low level 848-1037 Hz.
This tightly constrains symbol deviations to ensure inter-vendor compatibility.
Symbol rate accuracy: ≤10 PPM.

Modulation fidelity: rms Deviation Error ≤5% (Class A) or ≤10% (Class B).
For C4FM peak spurious frequency: 50-100 Hz (Class A) and 100-150 Hz (Class B).

Transmitter attack time (conventional): power ≤50 ms, encoder ≤100 ms.
With busy/idle: both power and encoder ≤30 ms.
Transmitter throughput delay: ≤125 ms.
RFSS throughput delay: ≤100 ms for simple repeaters.
RFSS idle-to-busy transition: ≤30 ms.

Conducted spurious emissions (VSWR): attenuated ≥50+10log(P) dB or 70 dB
whichever is less, tested at VSWR ≤3:1 (mobile), ≤6:1 (portable), ≤2:1
(base station).

TRUNKED SYSTEM TIMING (Section 3.3)

Control channel encode and RF power attack times depend on slot duration:
for 37.5 ms slots, encode attack is 2.00-4.15 ms; for 45 ms slots,
2.00-11.65 ms. RF power turn-off time ≤1.57 ms for both slot sizes.

Trunking request time: ≤160 ms (37.5 ms slot) or ≤167.5 ms (45 ms slot).
Time to grant in non-simulcast systems with single TSBK: ≤337.5-345.0 ms
(ISP slot dependent). In simulcast, single TSBK: ≤487.5-495.0 ms.
Transmitter key time on traffic channel: ≤150.0 ms (short form) or
≤171.1 ms (explicit channel form).

UNIT CHARACTERISTICS AND ENVIRONMENTAL (Section 3.4)

Temperature range: -30°C to +60°C (all equipment). At temperature extremes,
reference sensitivity may degrade up to 6 dB DFS at standard voltage, 8 dB
at ±10% voltage limits. Many other characteristics have no DFS (no degradation
from standard is allowed) including frequency accuracy, transmitter fidelity,
and symbol rate accuracy.

High humidity: 90-95% at +50°C. Performance requirements are similar to
temperature extremes.

Vibration (standard): two-part sinusoidal sweep, reference sensitivity, output
power, and frequency accuracy must hold with no DFS.

Shock: mobiles must survive 10g half-sine impacts (20g peak, 11 ms); portables
must survive 100 cm free-fall drops onto concrete on each of 6 faces.

Battery life (portables): ≥8 hours at standard duty cycle (6s TX or RX + 54s
standby).

Class A equipment must additionally pass MIL-STD-810E environmental tests:
rain (506.3), salt fog (509.3), sand/dust (510.3), functional shock (516.4),
and platform-specific vibration profiles for ground vehicles, helicopters,
propeller aircraft, and marine vessels.

==========================================================================
DOCUMENT HISTORY AND SCOPE EVOLUTION
==========================================================================

Initial publication (2000): Basic C4FM performance requirements.
CAAB-A (2002): Added 700 MHz band equipment limits.
CAAB-B (2004): Revised power line conducted limits per FCC.
CAAB-C (2010): Added simulcast modulation limits.
CAAB-D (2013): Added blocking rejection limits; revised offset adjacent
  channel rejection.
CAAB-E (2021): Added Broadband Strong Signal Intermodulation Rejection (SSIR)
  limits for multiple bands; miscellaneous clarifications.

==========================================================================
RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER DOCUMENTS
==========================================================================

This document is the performance limits standard; it is normatively paired
with TIA-102.CAAA-F (measurement methods). Both are required to qualify
equipment for P25 compliance. Physical layer requirements originate in
TIA-102.BAAA-B. FCC type acceptance incorporates these limits for 700 MHz
band equipment under Parts 27 and 90.

APCO/NASTD/FED Project 25 Steering Committee has adopted this document as
part of the P25 standard. The document is explicitly scoped to include both
public safety and non-public safety Private Land Mobile Radio applications.
