# P25 Analysis Notes

Value-add analysis that goes beyond what the TIA specs say on their own.
These pieces synthesize cross-document insights, correct common misconceptions,
and provide implementation guidance informed by real-world P25 system behavior.

These are **not** spec extractions — they are original analysis derived from
the TIA-102 specifications combined with practical engineering knowledge.

10. [AMBE Predictor State Separation Invariant](ambe_predictor_state_separation.md) — the three AMBE pipeline roles (decoder, encoder, rate converter) each maintain a prior-frame log-magnitude predictor. Coefficients often coincide (0.65 for half-rate intra-rate and for rate-converter cross-rate), but state instances must stay independent — sharing them produces AR-loop instability and ~50-frame spectral drift.
11. [AMBE Encoder Closed-Loop Predictor Feedback](ambe_encoder_closed_loop_predictor.md) — the encoder must run a matched decoder internally so its prior-frame predictor state tracks what the wire decoder will reconstruct, not what the encoder analyzed. Open-loop predictors look correct at frame 1 but drift over ~50 frames. Test: encoder and decoder predictor states must be bit-identical at every frame boundary.
12. [AMBE+2 Phase Regeneration Kernel as Discrete Hilbert Transform](ambe_phase_regen_hilbert.md) — US5701390's `h(m) = 2/(π·m)` is the discrete Hilbert transform impulse response. The regenerated phase is Kolmogorov's identity: phase = Hilbert{log|M̃|} for minimum-phase signals. Explains why this kernel, why γ = 0.44 scaling, why D = 19 truncation, and the ⌊L̃/4⌋ lower-harmonic exclusion.
13. [AMBE-3000 Rate-Index Table: Bit-Allocation and Naming Gaps](ambe3000_rate_bit_allocation_gap.md) — two coupled gaps: (1) per-field bit allocations for AMBE+2 chip rates 35–61 are not in public sources, requiring empirical characterization in `~/blip25-mbe` (open); (2) previously-used `r33`/`r34` aliases (P25 full-rate / half-rate) collided numerically with chip rate indices 33/34 (both half-rate variants) — **resolved 2026-04-16** by renaming aliases to `p25_fullrate` / `p25_halfrate` across the AMBE-3000 impl specs (matches blip25-mbe's existing module naming). CSVs follow chip indexing with P25 full-rate at project-extension rows 62/63.

[AMBE-3000 Multi-Subframe Rate Mapping](ambe3000_multi_subframe_rate_mapping.md) — the AMBE-3000F manual does not identify which chip rate indices use US6199037 multi-subframe joint quantization; bit rate alone cannot disambiguate 1-subframe vs 2-subframe encoding. 15. [AMBE-3000R as a BABA-A Decode Oracle: Chip-Side Caveats](ambe3000_chip_oracle_caveats.md) — the chip applies at least three proprietary post-synthesis behaviors (amplitude-dependent mute, internal ±10000 cap, stationary-signal detector sensitive to <1% jitter) that aren't in BABA-A §1.10–§1.12 and can't be disabled via DCMODE. Single-harmonic synthetic-MbeParams probes cannot recover clean `γ_w` or `M̃_l` scalars. Recommended methodology: multi-harmonic realistic-distribution magnitudes, accept ~2× absolute-scale uncertainty, treat any scalar as "consistent with spec" rather than ground truth.
16. [FDMA PDU Frame: Unified View](fdma_pdu_frame.md) — DUID `0xC` carries six payload families (TSDU, MBT Standard, MBT Alternative, Unconfirmed Data, Confirmed Data, Response); the 5-bit Format field plus SAP select the subtype. Resolves the `0x15` dual-use (Unconfirmed Data vs. Standard MBT) and the DUID `0x7` vs. `0xC` TSBK ambiguity with a single dispatcher pseudocode, byte-level worked examples for each family (including pad-boundary last-block layouts for Confirmed and Unconfirmed), a Header-CRC-16-failure recovery strategy table (§6.1), and a consolidated CRC summary (Header CRC-16, per-block CRC-9, Packet CRC-32).
17. [Passive Cross-Copy PDU Assembly](passive_cross_copy_pdu_assembly.md) — Motorola repeat-broadcast redundantly transmits CAD / dispatch PDUs for time diversity; a passive decoder can cross-merge CRC-clean blocks across physical copies to recover PDUs that no single copy carried cleanly. Implementer heuristics for session identity, idle timeout, emission semantics, and partial-session handling, with the merge procedure and failure-mode table. Not a spec amendment — passive assembly lives outside BAAA-B / BAED-A's active-receiver ARQ model.

[Passive Receiver Confidence Labels](passive_receiver_confidence_labels.md) — four-label taxonomy (`native_clean` / `corrected` / `merged` / `partial`) for the confidence score a passive decoder surfaces per PDU, with rules, promotion order, per-block composition, and reference struct field mapping. Sits above the BAAA-B CRC integrity primitives; gives downstream consumers (dispatch UIs, SIEM, benchmarking) a common vocabulary instead of every project inventing its own.
19. [Motorola SNDCP N-PDU Preamble (Trunked IV&D)](motorola_sndcp_npdu_preamble.md) — **SNDCP sits at `ip_offset - 2`**, directly in front of the IPv4 version nibble, and decodes cleanly under standard BAEB-C Figure 26 semantics. Everything between the BAEB-C reassembled-payload boundary and `ip_offset - 2` is LLC user-plane overhead (TIA-102.BAED-A). Earlier `0x40 0x06 ...` mystery was LLC bytes being read as if they were SNDCP — retired. Covers the full LLC → SNDCP → IPv4 → UDP layering, the 30-byte full-uncompressed IPv4+UDP+SNDCP budget, RFC 2507 compression exceptions (broadcast / encrypted always uncompressed), the scan-for-IPv4-then-back-align-SNDCP heuristic, and the one remaining open question (exact LLC user-plane byte layout).
20. [Motorola Conventional SCEP vs Trunked SNDCP](motorola_conventional_scep_vs_trunked_sndcp.md) — conventional and trunked ASTRO 25 do not use the same data-encapsulation protocol. **SCEP is a TIA-102 standard (defined in TIA-102.BAEB-B §4), not Motorola-proprietary** — Motorola's "Simple CAI Encapsulation Protocol" is an alternate name for TIA's "Simple Convergence Encapsulation Protocol". SCEP's data-carrying form is **zero-wrapper** (IP datagram is placed directly in a CAI logical message with Data Header Offset = 0); SCEP ARP is the 22-octet LLID↔IPv4 binding mechanism. Trunked IV&D uses SNDCP + LLC on BAEB-C.
21. [TSBK Bit Tables vs Pseudo-Layouts](trunking_tsbk_bit_tables_vs_pseudo_layouts.md) — why the AABC-E implementation spec §4.3 / §5.3 field layouts are now bit-indexed tables instead of octet-oriented pseudo-layouts. The PDF's 2D bit tables are authoritative; text extractions that collapse them to octet prose routinely lose 24-bit Source Address fields, conflate 4+12-bit band+channel pairs into 16-bit "channel" fields, and hide AIV/EX/RC discriminated unions in narrative prose. Gap 0020 documents seven live decoder bugs this pattern caused before the rewrite.
23. [DVSI Foundational MBE Patents — Public-Domain Implementation References](ambe_foundational_patents.md) — extracts implementation-quality algorithmic content from three expired DVSI patents: US 5,081,681 (phase synthesis from fundamental + V/UV + random component), US 5,664,051 (decoder architecture, "don't transmit phase, save bits" insight), and US 5,870,405 (the **complete P25 Phase 1 IMBE FEC patent** — Golay/Hamming parity matrices, bit prioritization, pseudo-random bit modulation generator, frame error/repeat/mute logic, adaptive smoothing, all explicitly tied to "APCO/NASTD/Fed Project 25 Vocoder Description" appendices). All three patents are public domain (expired 2010–2016) and provide closed-form equations and tables that can be directly transcribed into a clean-room IMBE implementation.

25. [P25 NID — MMDVMHost Cross-Reference Audit](p25_nid_implementation_audit.md) — third-party validation of BAAA-B Impl Spec §3.1/§3.2/§3.3 against `g4klx/MMDVMHost` `P25NID.cpp` + `BCH.cpp`. All 48 BCH(63,16,23) generator-polynomial coefficients match; 64-bit NID layout (NAC/DUID/parity/overall-P) matches; per-DUID overall-parity-bit table matches and **disambiguates** the previously-open DUID `0x7` (TSBK) entry to **P = 0**. Surfaces a new architectural pattern not previously covered: MMDVMHost decodes by Hamming-distance lookup against a pre-encoded 7-codeword table (one per DUID, all sharing the configured NAC), exploiting BCH linearity to achieve the same t = 11 correction floor as an algebraic decoder with no syndrome math. Correct only for known-NAC receivers (transceivers); a passive scanner needs algebraic BCH for NAC discovery and can layer the table approach as a hot-path optimization for tracked NACs. Includes a draft §3.3.2 informative subsection for the impl spec.
