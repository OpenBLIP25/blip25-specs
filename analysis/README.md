# P25 Analysis Notes

Value-add analysis that goes beyond what the TIA specs say on their own.
These pieces synthesize cross-document insights, correct common misconceptions,
and provide implementation guidance informed by real-world P25 system behavior.

These are **not** spec extractions — they are original analysis derived from
the TIA-102 specifications combined with practical engineering knowledge.

[Analysis Encoder Addendum: Eq. 7 `r(t)` Transcription Error](vocoder_analysis_eq7_correction.md) — the addendum's §0.3.2 / §0.3.8 / §0.3.9 originally squared `s_LPF` on both factors of Eq. 7, breaking dimensional analysis of Eq. 5 and producing `E(P)` excursions of 10⁴–10⁶ on voiced content. 10. [AMBE Predictor State Separation Invariant](ambe_predictor_state_separation.md) — the three AMBE pipeline roles (decoder, encoder, rate converter) each maintain a prior-frame log-magnitude predictor. Coefficients often coincide (0.65 for half-rate intra-rate and for rate-converter cross-rate), but state instances must stay independent — sharing them produces AR-loop instability and ~50-frame spectral drift.
11. [AMBE Encoder Closed-Loop Predictor Feedback](ambe_encoder_closed_loop_predictor.md) — the encoder must run a matched decoder internally so its prior-frame predictor state tracks what the wire decoder will reconstruct, not what the encoder analyzed. Open-loop predictors look correct at frame 1 but drift over ~50 frames. Test: encoder and decoder predictor states must be bit-identical at every frame boundary.
12. [AMBE+2 Phase Regeneration Kernel as Discrete Hilbert Transform](ambe_phase_regen_hilbert.md) — US5701390's `h(m) = 2/(π·m)` is the discrete Hilbert transform impulse response. The regenerated phase is Kolmogorov's identity: phase = Hilbert{log|M̃|} for minimum-phase signals. Explains why this kernel, why γ = 0.44 scaling, why D = 19 truncation, and the ⌊L̃/4⌋ lower-harmonic exclusion.
13. [AMBE-3000 Rate-Index Table: Bit-Allocation and Naming Gaps](ambe3000_rate_bit_allocation_gap.md) — two coupled gaps: (1) per-field bit allocations for AMBE+2 chip rates 35–61 are not in public sources, requiring empirical characterization in `~/blip25-mbe` (open); (2) previously-used `r33`/`r34` aliases (P25 full-rate / half-rate) collided numerically with chip rate indices 33/34 (both half-rate variants) — **resolved 2026-04-16** by renaming aliases to `p25_fullrate` / `p25_halfrate` across the AMBE-3000 impl specs (matches blip25-mbe's existing module naming). CSVs follow chip indexing with P25 full-rate at project-extension rows 62/63.
14. [AMBE-3000 Multi-Subframe Rate Mapping](ambe3000_multi_subframe_rate_mapping.md) — the AMBE-3000F manual does not identify which chip rate indices use US6199037 multi-subframe joint quantization; bit rate alone cannot disambiguate 1-subframe vs 2-subframe encoding. Impl specs' existing `r17`/`r18` examples are unverified (and those chip indices are AMBE-2000 generation, not AMBE+2). New `multi_subframe_rates.csv` marks 60 of 64 rows pending; decoder §10.5, encoder §10.2, and rate converter §10.4 describe the algorithm independent of rate-index mapping. Resolution via blip25-mbe characterization.
15. [AMBE-3000R as a BABA-A Decode Oracle: Chip-Side Caveats](ambe3000_chip_oracle_caveats.md) — the chip applies at least three proprietary post-synthesis behaviors (amplitude-dependent mute, internal ±10000 cap, stationary-signal detector sensitive to <1% jitter) that aren't in BABA-A §1.10–§1.12 and can't be disabled via DCMODE. Single-harmonic synthetic-MbeParams probes cannot recover clean `γ_w` or `M̃_l` scalars. Recommended methodology: multi-harmonic realistic-distribution magnitudes, accept ~2× absolute-scale uncertainty, treat any scalar as "consistent with spec" rather than ground truth.
16. [FDMA PDU Frame: Unified View](fdma_pdu_frame.md) — DUID `0xC` carries six payload families (TSDU, MBT Standard, MBT Alternative, Unconfirmed Data, Confirmed Data, Response); the 5-bit Format field plus SAP select the subtype. Resolves the `0x15` dual-use (Unconfirmed Data vs. Standard MBT) and the DUID `0x7` vs. `0xC` TSBK ambiguity with a single dispatcher pseudocode, byte-level worked examples for each family, and a consolidated CRC summary (Header CRC-16, per-block CRC-9, Packet CRC-32).
