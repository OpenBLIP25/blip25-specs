This document is the authoritative list of P25 standards currently recognized by the
P25 Steering Committee. It is used here as the baseline inventory for tracking which
P25 documents exist today.

## Column definitions

The source PDF has these columns:

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Official Document Title** | Human-readable title of the standard. |
| **TIA Document #** | TIA-102 document number (the canonical identifier). `TIA-102.xxx` = normative standard, `TSB-102.xxx` = Telecommunications Systems Bulletin (informative). Trailing letter = revision (A, B, C…); `-N` = numbered addendum. |
| **ANSI** | An `X` indicates that the TIA-102 document has **also been adopted by ANSI as an American National Standard** (i.e., it is published as `ANSI/TIA-102.xxx`). TIA is an ANSI-accredited Standards Development Organization, but not every TIA standard is separately submitted/approved as an ANSI standard — only those marked `X` here carry ANSI status. A blank cell means the document is a TIA-only standard. |
| **Published** | Month/year TIA published the (current) revision. |
| **Re-affirmed** | Month/year TIA formally re-affirmed the existing revision without changes. |
| **Approved by the P25 Steering Committee as a P25 Standard** | Month/year the P25 SC adopted this TIA document as an official P25 standard. TIA publication and P25 SC acceptance are separate events — a TIA standard is not automatically a P25 standard. |
| **Comments** | Status notes (e.g. "Draft in Progress", "Cancelled", "Absorbed into …"). |

Notation in date cells: `MMM-YY` (e.g. `Apr-19` = April 2019). A few cells use day-month
format (`11-Jul`, `13-Feb`) exactly as they appear in the source.

## Notes on the ANSI column (answering the original question)

Yes — an `X` in the ANSI column means that document **is also an ANSI standard**. TIA's
TR-8 committee is an ANSI-accredited Standards Development Organization, so a TIA-102
standard marked `X` here has been balloted and adopted by ANSI as an American National
Standard and is properly cited as `ANSI/TIA-102.xxx`. TIA documents without the `X` are
TIA-only (still valid TIA standards, but not separately designated as ANSI standards).

Counts in this baseline: **~25 documents carry ANSI designation** out of ~105 approved
P25 standards, concentrated in the foundational air-interface, trunking control, vocoder,
data-services, location-services, and transceiver-performance groups — i.e. the areas
most often cited in procurement and regulatory contexts where ANSI status matters.
